Interactive Experience Research

BrooomStick by Christina Bertman

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The main aspect that stood out to me when first viewing the website was the interesting aesthetics and unique use of illustrations in addition to the interact aspects of the experience when scrolling.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Hovering over the navigation bar up the top Scrolling up and down to see how the PNG rubbish mans animation reacts to scrolling. Spending some time to read and take in the comments of the green group of people in the background. Finding out that this website is related to janitors or tradespeople Scrolling back up to the top of the website and clicking the profession section in the navigation bar to find the links link to different sections of the same website. Reading and learning more about the role of janitor. Observing the textures of the background and the different animations as I continue to scroll.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

One of the main experiences I spent the most time interacting with was scrolling animations with the trash, observing how they flowed in and out of their initial and final positions as I scrolled up and down the page.

4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

While spending 2 minutes initially interacting with the website the most common action was slowly scrolling and interacting with the different rubbish animations. These animations are unique and captured my attention straight away.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The websites main objective is to educate its audience about the prior views of a janitor and why their job is so important. This is expressed through an interactive and engaging experience. The website is laid out in a way that splits up information making it easier for the user to take in. Using different forms of communication the author publishes information in an entertaining and informative way.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The website's fundamental goal is to publish information about a janitor's importance through their history. Christina Bertmans use of interactivity translates to people being more engaged and staying for more time with the website. Its creative forms of communication, including speech bubbles, articles and small fact bubbles, break down content into more digestible fragments making it much more inviting to the user. Its interactive user interface creates an easily navigable website with the desire to see what animations lie ahead keeping the user's attention alive.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The different scrolling patterns that are utilized by Christina Bertman force the user to stay with the content for a while before scrolling further. An example is the transition from vertical scrolling to horizontal scrolling when we are presented with artworks. This sudden change in the scroll pattern is unexpected, this captures the user's attention and semi-forces them to stop and identify what is important enough to lead to a change in the scrolling pattern. We see different scrolling behaviours throughout the website, which is one of the reasons why the website retains the users engagement.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

For some users, the interactivity of the website may lead to frustration. To avoid this Bertman has added a navigation bar feature, acting as a shortcut for users, either returning or wishing to navigate to a certain aspect of the website promptly, without needing to navigate throughout the entirety of the website multiple times.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

As mentioned previously the website has many methods of communication and these are predominantly seen in the form of digital media. However, through digital media, there is an expression or what may be called an adaptation of print media or collage more specifically. This acts as the foundation for the aesthetics of the website and serves as an effective communication method. Combining the animated collage with the themes present further demonstrates the effectiveness of user engagement.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

As the site uses print media and collage styling, the website may be considered art by some, and being art, it creates certain reactions, different reactions from everyone. Because of this, it is hard to decipher how the user will react when viewing the website. What we do know is that the website attempts to be engaging through interactive elements and creative input. From this, we can assume that Bertmans work was meant to be appreciated not just for the information on site but for the site itself.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The website communicates the importance and the value of professions with a focus on janitors. This is reflected in finding a sense of empathy amongst its users. Viewing how they are mistreated given the importance they have in keeping our world clean. An example of how the website expresses how janitors are mistreated is demonstrated by the janitors being represented as PNG, symbolising their invisibility in the real world.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating aspect of the website experience is the inability to quickly navigate the website. Because the website is laid out in a storytelling type of way, navigating up and down the page has proven to be difficult and inefficient. At the top of the website, there is a navigation bar which does help, however, once the user begins scrolling down the navigation bar disappears leaving the only method of navigation being scrolling. Another slight frustration is the slow performance given the accessibility that some users may have. These slow loading times can test users frustration, especially given today's expectation of fast-paced responses surrounding digital media this can be frustrating.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying aspect of the website is its aesthetic appeal and animations. The interface has unique aesthetics making the website visually appealing. The colours, font and aesthetic style all contribute to creating a positive emotional response. The website has consistent design elements allowing users to predict the behaviour of the website. Through the animation and illustrations used we get a idea of what the themes of the website are without reading anything.
